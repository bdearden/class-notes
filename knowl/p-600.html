Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} a_{ij} \end{bmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{bmatrix} b_{ij} \end{bmatrix}\) be two \(m\times n\) binary matrices. We define:

  • The join of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A \lor B\text{,}\) to be the \(m \times n\) matrix whose \(ij\)th entry is \(a_{ij} \lor b_{ij}\text{.}\)
  • The meet of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A \land B\text{,}\) to be the \(m \times n\) matrix whose \(ij\)th entry is \(a_{ij} \land b_{ij}\text{.}\)
