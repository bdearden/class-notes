Definition 2.3.3.

Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} a_{ij} \end{bmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{bmatrix} b_{ij} \end{bmatrix}\) be two \(m\times n\) matrices. We define their sum by:

\begin{equation*} A + B = \begin{bmatrix} a_{ij} + b_{ij} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

That is, we add the corresponding entries together. The result is also an \(m\times n\) matrix.

Let \(A\) be an \(m\times k\) matrix, and let \(B\) be a \(k\times n\) matrix. Then the product \(AB = \begin{bmatrix}c_{ij} \end{bmatrix}\) is defined by:

\begin{equation*} c_{ij} = a_{i1}\cdot b_{1j} + a_{i2}\cdot b_{2j} + \dots + a_{ik}\cdot b_{kj} \end{equation*}
